Sequences & Series
Arithmetic sequences and series
Image credit goes to: Mathematical Mysteries
Arithmetic sequences concern common intervals either being added or subtracted from one term to the next. These sequences are extremely helpful in repeated additions of numbers and understanding certain linear relationships. There are some key terms which I will define. Firstly, the common difference is the interval between terms (basically the amount being added or subtracted). The 1st term is the first term of the sequence while the 0th term represents the "projected" 0th term of the Sequence if the sequence had gone on. The letter n represents the term number.
Geometric sequences & practice problems
Finite geometric sequences are all you will need for most competition mathematics. However, infinite geometric series could appear in certain questions from time to time. Infinite geometric sequences are much easier than their finite counterpart, with the sum of a geometric sequence being represented by a/(1-r) for any r that is less than 1. Overall, sequences and series are a large part of discussion and are very important in many different branches of mathematics. You will definetly use sequences at a certain point in your mathematics journey, and they will be immensely helpful in many different problems.
